Formula 1 Teams

Author: BadBunnY | Date: Nov 20, 2024

1. Scuderia Ferrari
The oldest and most prestigious team in Formula 1, holding 16 Constructors' Championships and 15 Drivers' Championships. Ferrari has competed in every season since the championship's inception in 1950, making it a cornerstone of the sport.

2. Red Bull Racing
A dominant force since 2005, known for innovative car designs and securing multiple Constructors' and Drivers' Championships, particularly excelling during the 2010s and 2020s. Red Bull has become synonymous with performance and cutting-edge technology.

3. Mercedes-AMG Petronas
A powerhouse since its return as a works team in 2010, dominating the turbo-hybrid era from 2014 with numerous consecutive Constructors' and Drivers' Championships. Mercedes continues to set benchmarks for excellence in the sport.

4. McLaren
An iconic team founded in 1966, with 8 Constructors' Championships and 12 Drivers' Championships to its name. Known for its rich history, innovation, and legendary drivers, McLaren remains a key player in Formula 1.

5. Aston Martin
Rebranded from Racing Point in 2021, Aston Martin has a growing presence in Formula 1. Though yet to win a Constructors' or Drivers' Championship, the team is ambitious and aims to challenge the top teams with its strong foundation.

6. Alpine
Formerly Renault, the team has been active in various forms since 1977. Alpine boasts 2 Constructors' Championships and 2 Drivers' Championships, notably achieving success with back-to-back titles in the mid-2000s.

7. Haas
The newest team on the Formula 1 grid, debuting in 2016. While Haas has not yet won a Constructors' or Drivers' Championship, the team remains determined, striving to grow and establish a consistent presence in the midfield.

8. Alfa Romeo
A historic name in Formula 1, Alfa Romeo won the first Drivers' Championship in 1950. Returning to the sport in 2019 under a partnership with Sauber, Alfa Romeo continues to build on its legacy in the modern era.

9. AlphaTauri
Previously known as Toro Rosso, AlphaTauri has been Red Bull's sister team since 2006. Though primarily a development team, it secured a historic victory at the 2008 Italian Grand Prix and continues to fight for consistent midfield finishes.

10. Williams
